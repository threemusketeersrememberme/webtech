REMEMBER ME

WHY YOU SHOULD REMEMBER TO INCLUDE SAN MIGUEL IN YOUR BULACAN BUCKET LIST
by three musketeers
SEE OUR PHOTOS
    Bulacan, being home to most of the Philippines’ national heroes, is deemed to be one of the prestige historical places in the Philippines. However, how much do you know about its history? A few from the past Hekasi or Philippine History lessons, maybe. But do you still remember what, when and where the first historical moment in Bulacan happened? Probably, not. Well then, the first notable historical moment in this province that ended the first phase of the Philippine Revolution during the Spaniards’ reign on 1897 is the Pact of Biak Na Bato or in Tagalog, Kasuduan sa Biak na Bato, which is in the municipality of San Miguel, Bulacan. Cool, right? But what about it?  

    We just want to introduce the chief municipality, in terms of history, San Miguel, Bulacan. Just something nice to know!

    On this note, we, the three musketeers, advocate to raise everyone’s awareness of this rural municipality in Bulacan; as we seek to unravel the many natural wonders, historical places, and opulent culture it holds. One of the advocates, Christina Cambiado, is a folk of San Miguel, herself and raised her concern that most Filipinos, even Bulakenyos from other municipalities, see San Miguel as a plain rural village ( Julianne from Bocaue and Yvonne from Bustos can attest to that) when in fact, there’s so much more to discover! Thus leading us to venture out to San Miguel, and to tell you, my friends, we were not disappointed and we’re definitely going back for a longer trip!

    After our one-day adventure, we made a list of reasons on why you should include San Miguel in your bucket list ( which we hope could convince you!) if ever you want to spend a holiday in Bulacan.

1. Trace back our History

Feeling “makabayan”?

    Well, San Miguel is the home of Trinidad Tecson, one of the renowned female revolutionaries of the Philippine Revolution, the Mother of Biak-na-Bato, and also known as 'The Mother of Philippine Red Cross' and 'Mother of Mercy'. And up ‘till now, her house, the Tecson House, still stands tall. Of course, you can pay a visit in her house, too!

    Additionally, as you have read in the introduction, Biak na Bato is situated in San Miguel, Bulacan. This famous tourist spot is a 40 minute ride from the city proper. (we will add more info pag punta po namin hehe)

    Aside from visiting these places, you could also have a recall on history from tales and stories boasted by the folks about the municipality’s role in history. Some of which are, the hiding of the HukBaLaHap in San Miguel, that it is home to Maximo Viola, the financier of Jose Rizal’s Noli Me Tangere, and locals’ conspiracy tales that the Tecsons are those who really funded the publishing of the novel as they are richer than the Violas, that’s why traces of Tecsons’ are still living until now while Violas’ are wiped out.

    Trust me, you would learn a month’s worth of history lessons in a day through the locals!

2. Inhale fresh air

    Nature-lover? Feeling adventurous? Or just simply wanting to have some peace of mind in place with good landscape and fresh air? All three? Today’s your lucky day, dear friend, ‘cause you’re going to know that San Miguel can satisfy your pleasures!

    Aside from Biak-na-Bato, this municipality takes pride of other excellent nature spots. Fond of hiking? San Miguel takes pride of Mt. Gola and Mt. Manalmon, which you could take for a twin hike! According to mountaineers, these mountains are great for beginners and the estimated time to reach the summit per mountain is two hours. Piece of cake? Then you can go spelunking through the Madlum Cave and Bayukbok Cave, too! Bayukbok Cave is divided into two, termed Bayukbok 1 & 2, wherein you can experience different challenges like crawling, rappelling, helicopter ladder-climbing, and some crawling again, and crossing the monkey bridge and of course, don’t miss the chance to go for a swim in the clean, running river! 

    Unfortunately, we were not able to hike and spelunk yet due to hectic school schedules and tasks, but we’ll definitely back and maybe we’ll see you around!

3. Eat new dishes and have taste of their famous pastillas

    Honestly, when we hear San Miguel, the first thing that comes to our mind is pastillas, though we, Yvonne and Julianne particularly, never had a taste of authentic pastillas from this municipality. So when we had the chance to get a taste of their famous carabao-milk-pastillas, ugh, this must be the taste of heaven, was what I thought of, no sugar-coating (pun intended)!
 When it comes to viands, they are famed for ‘Soup 5’ which is garnished with the cow's genitals. It may sound weird while you’re reading it, but if you get a taste of it, you’ll definitely forget your name.

    Conversely, San Miguel also has the fried one-day-old chick as a street food, so if you’re up to exotic treats, you should go and try these!
 
    These are only 3 of the plenty of reasons why you should go to San Miguel, Bulacan we discovered for our one-day travel, note for only a day, how much more if you try to stay there for a week or so, right? So, if ever you paid a visit to San Miguel and thought of a reason to visit it aside from these, please let us know in the comments below! Have a joyous and safe tripping out there guys!
